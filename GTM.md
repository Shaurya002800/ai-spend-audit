# GTM.md

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." Specifically: **engineering managers and CTOs at Series A–B SaaS companies with 10–50 engineers**, who have recently onboarded multiple AI coding tools across their team and are starting to see the bill. Secondary: **indie hackers and solo founders** running lean who can't afford to overpay for tools they don't fully use.

What they have in common: they're the person who approved the Cursor or Copilot subscription, they now see the bill, they have a vague feeling they might be overpaying, but no benchmark and no time to research it themselves.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days with $0 Budget

**Week 1:**
1. Post "Show HN: SpendLens — free AI spend audit for startups" on Hacker News at 9am ET on a weekday. The tool is genuinely useful and non-commercial-feeling; it should do well.
2. Cold DM 20 founders on X who have recently posted about AI tool costs. No pitch — just "I built this, thought you'd find it useful: [link]." Target accounts with <5k followers (they read DMs).
3. Post in Cursor's Discord in the #general channel: "built a free tool to check if your team is on the right Cursor plan — happy to share." Community-oriented framing.

**Week 2:**
4. Post in Rands Leadership Slack #tools channel: "Found myself manually comparing AI tool pricing every quarter — built this instead."
5. Write a short blog post: "The 4 most common ways startups overpay for AI tools in 2026" — publish on dev.to. Each point links to SpendLens as the way to check your own stack. Dev.to has 1M+ monthly readers and the post will rank for comparison queries.
6. Submit to Ben's Bites, TLDR Tech, and The Rundown AI newsletters (free submissions). These newsletters reach 100k+ founders and engineers each.

**Week 3–4:**
7. If HN post worked: reply to every comment, find the highest-upvoted comments from engineering managers, DM them with "would love your feedback on the recommendations for your stack."
8. Find 3 indie hackers with newsletters who talk about SaaS tooling. Offer them a personalized audit of their own stack for free in exchange for a mention.
9. Post an honest "what I learned auditing 50 AI stacks" tweet thread with anonymized data from real audits. Data is compelling; thread format spreads.
