# GTM.md

## Who this is for

The clearest user for this product is someone on a small or mid-sized technical team who is now responsible for AI tool spend and has a suspicion that the stack got messy.

More specifically:

- engineering managers
- CTOs at smaller SaaS companies
- founders who approve software spend themselves
- indie hackers who try lots of tools and forget to clean up later

The common thread is not "likes AI." It is "now has a bill and wants a fast answer."

## Where I think they would find it

If I were trying to get the first real users, I would start in places where people already complain about tooling costs or compare plans:

- Hacker News
- X / Twitter
- Indie Hackers
- engineering Slack groups
- product and dev communities around Cursor, Claude, Copilot, and similar tools

The strongest hook is not "here is another AI app." The strongest hook is "this tells you if you are wasting money."

## First distribution moves I would try

I would keep the early GTM pretty simple:

1. Post the project on Hacker News with a clear, plain-English title.
2. Share it directly with people who have recently talked about AI tool costs.
3. Write one short post breaking down the most common ways teams overspend.
4. Use actual audit patterns from early users to sharpen the messaging.

This project probably does better with practical framing than with hype.

## What would make it work

I think the product only spreads if the output feels specific enough to forward to someone else. If a user sees "you could save $420/month by dropping two overlapping tools," that is shareable. If they just get generic optimization language, it dies.

So the GTM depends a lot on product clarity. The better the result page is, the easier distribution becomes.
